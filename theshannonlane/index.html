About

The story of my life.

  • An Early Beginning

    My appreciation for technology started at the early age of two. I have the privilege to say I am a second generation geek because of my father. I started out sitting on his lap just toggling with the mouse and was mesmerized by the colors on the screens. Little did I know I was a very young UI tester for the K-12 software for his company. Growing up with technology always at my figure tips has allowed me to witness and appreciate the evolution of software, and see it become more user friendly.

  • Cardboard Creations

    With duct tape and cardboard, my passion for building started at a young age. As a young child if I desired a toy, I made it; from a doll house, to a cash register, to Santa’s sleigh, and even a ‘computer.' If I could find a piece of cardboard, I could make anything I desired. With just a roll of duct tape and a simple cardboard box, my imagination would run wild.

  • A Company Was Born

    While attending high school I learned how to crochet but never imaged that my simple hobbit would become profitable. I started out making a headband with earflaps called a Broyo. It turned into me making them for a friend, then a friend of a friend, and then the whole high school. My demand became so high I was able to recruit two employees to help with the load. Overall, my creativity and drive allowed me to create a small business and learn what it takes to run it as a teenager.

  • Finding The Correlation

    From my earliest memories my favorite subject has always been math. During college I attended two undergraduate research experiences. Through research I found out how hard it is to solve for the unknown but at the same time so exciting. I was able to challenge myself intellectually to think outside the box to prove my theory.

  • Finding My Stride

    In my senior year of college I found myself in an introductory computer science course. It was there where my world got a little bit bigger. I became much more aware of where my innovative and analytical self could meet my creative and driven self halfway. It is in the realm of computer science and software development where I can create whatever I desire; I could be creative and analytical in my approaches, and drive towards creating great applications for not only myself, but many others.

Saint Paul Athletic Club

Mobile & Website Design

PROBLEM: An efficient and functional solution for the members of Saint Paul Athletic Club to easily obtain class schedules and social events information.
STEPS TO SOLUTION: I started tackling this problem through conversations. I talked to the management at the club and members to get their idea of what a successful app would look like. From there I put their’ s and my ideas to paper.

MOBILE DESIGN

WEB DESIGN

I did not produce a very sophisticated wire frame but it was enough to make a prototype. The prototype lacked some of the main functionality features but it was enough for the management to sign off on the design. I worked with my developer to add in features to cater to any age user and create a sleek website for the club's management to be able to edit the information displayed in the app.
SOLUTION:

  • Efficiency - It was clear to be able to achieve the goal the schedules would need to update without forcing the user to manually update from the app store. Thus, the app dynamically refreshes with external data that is easily updated by the club.
  • Consistency - It was important to maintain the same feel as the club's current website. To do this, their fonts, color schemes, and images were implemented in the mobile app.
  • Minimalistic - A main purpose of this app was to reduce users frustrations with viewing the current class schedules and social events through the mobile website. Thus, it was important for the look to be simplistic but functional.
  • Flexibility - This app was tailored to any aged user. One being the bottom buttons can be touch or swipe left/right on the pages will both cause the same functionality of navigating between the three main pages. The other feature is closing the information boxes for class schedule times. A user can click on classes or events to get more information. At this point a popup box appears on their screen, and to exit the screen, a user can click the ‘Close’ button at the bottom of the page, or can simply click the background outside of the popup box.

The Bubble Bounce

Mobile Design

PROBLEM: Create an app that intrigued users to continue to play.
STEPS TO SOLUTION: This application was driven by the developer. I started with talking to him about what was the main functionality of the app. It was simple, pop two matching color bubbles before too many bubble fill the screen. From there I started to come up with color combinations I thought would work for the app. Between the developer and the QA testers we decided on final design.
SOLUTION:

  • Responsive - I wanted to make sure the bubbles moved at the right speed for the user. If the bubbles moved too fast the user's touch would not be recognized by the program but if they moved too slow the user would not be challenged enough to continue to play. It was important to make sure the touch recognition was just right or else a user would become frustrated./li>
  • Minimalistic - My goal for this application was to keep the colors light like bubbles but not too much like the colors one might see around the Easter season. I asked our testers to help me figure out what they liked to get the right colors. It took many different iterations of fonts and colors to get to where it is today.
  • Realistic - For the bubbles to collide correctly and look appealing to the user was important to me. During development some balls would get stuck together and not move apart. I worked the developer to come up with creative ideas to handle this functionality which is what is used in the game today.
