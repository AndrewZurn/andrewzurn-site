Saint Paul Athletic Club

Mobile & Website Design

PROBLEM: An efficient and functionable solition for the members of Saint Paul Athletic Club to easily obtain class schedules and social events.
STEPS TO SOLUTION: I started tackling this problem through conversations. I talked to the managment at the club and members to get their idea of what a succefull app would look like. From there I put their's and my ideas to paper.

MOBILE DESIGN

WEB DESIGN

I did not produce a very sophiscated wireform but enough to make a prototype. The protype lacked some of the main functionality features but it was enough for the mamagment to sign off on the design. I worked with my developer to add in features to cater to any age user and create a sleek website for the club's manament to be able to edit the class schedules and social events.
SOLUTION:

  • Efficienty - It was clear to be able to achieve the goal the schedules would need to update without forcing the user to manually update from the app store. Thus, the app dynmically refreshes with external data that is easily updated by the club.
  • Consistancy - It was important to maintain the same feel as the club's current website. To do this, their fonts, color schemes, and images were implemented in the mobile app.
  • Minilistic - A main purpose of this app was to reduce users fustrations with viewing the current class schedules and social events through the mobile website. Thus, it was important for the look to be simlistic but functionable.
  • Flexiablity - This app was tailored to any aged user. One being the bottom buttons can be touch or swipe left/right on the pages will both cause the same functionality of navigating between the three main pages. The other feature is closing the information boxes for class schedule times. A user can click on classes or events to get more information. At this point a popup box appears on their screen, and to exit the screen, a user can click a Close button at the bottom of the page, or can simply click the background outside of the popup box.

The Bubble Bounce

Mobile Design

This was my second mobile app I designed. This application is simple, pop two matching color bubbles before too many bubble fill the screen.
My goal for this application was to keep the colors light like bubbles but not too much like the colors one might see around the Easter season. I asked our testers to help me figure out what they liked to get the right colors. It took many different iterations of fonts and colors to get to where it is today.
I also worked hard on making sure the bubbles moved at the right speed for the user. If the bubbles moved too fast they touch would not be recognized by the program but if they moved too slow the user would not be challenged enough to continue to play. It was important to make sure the touch recognition was just right or else a user would become frustrated.
Another feature I worked to improve was the collisions of the bubbles. For the bubbles to collide correctly and look appealing to the user was important to me. During development some balls would get stuck together and not move apart. I worked the developer to come up with creative ideas to handle this functionality which is what is used in the game today.


BridgeFront, LLC

Mobile Design

The first mobile app I designed was for Bridgefront LLC. The app allows users to take a HIPAA certified course and receive a certificate upon successful course completion.
The importance of this app was the contents on the page. I wanted it to look similar to the online course, so I started there with font ideas. From there little tweaks were made from the sizes on the different devices.
Another feature to this app is the bookmark capability. Since the user is choosing to take this course on their iPhone or iPad, they are more likely to complete the course in multiple sessions. The app allows the user to leave off where they started, or even navigate through the course hierarchy if they are looking for a specific chapter.

Flyer Design
